<p><p>Voice prostheses are medical devices used for patients who have undergone laryngectomies to help them produce speech. There are two main types of voice prostheses: indwelling and non-indwelling. Indwelling voice prostheses are implanted into the tracheoesophageal puncture and remain permanently in place, while non-indwelling voice prostheses are interchangeable and require regular replacement. Both types serve the same purpose of allowing patients to speak by directing airflow from the lungs to the esophagus.</p></p>
