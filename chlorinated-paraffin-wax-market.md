<p><p>Chlorinated paraffin wax is categorized into different types based on the chlorine content, including L-grade (chlorine content of 40-50%), M-grade (chlorine content of 50-60%), and H-grade (chlorine content of 70% or higher). L-grade chlorinated paraffin wax is commonly used in metalworking fluids and as a plasticizer in rubber. M-grade is used in flame retardant applications, while H-grade is favored for its high flame retardant properties and is extensively used in coatings, adhesives, and sealants. Each grade caters to different industrial needs and requirements.</p></p>
