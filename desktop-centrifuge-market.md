<p><p>Desktop centrifuges are available in two main types based on their maximum operating speed: low speed and high speed. Low-speed centrifuges typically operate at speeds below 10,000 rotations per minute (rpm) and are suitable for routine laboratory tasks such as separating blood components or isolating DNA. High-speed centrifuges, on the other hand, operate at speeds above 10,000 rpm and are more powerful, making them ideal for more complex applications such as protein purification or particle separation. Both types offer efficient and reliable performance for various laboratory needs.</p></p>
