<p><p>Medical traction devices are used to treat orthopedic conditions by applying a pulling force to bones and muscles. There are two main types of medical traction devices: Skeletal traction devices, which involve inserting pins or wires into the bone to provide a strong and stable pull, and Skin traction devices, which use adhesive straps or wraps to apply a lighter and more gentle pull on the skin surface. Both types of traction devices are important tools in orthopedic treatment and can help aid in the healing process of various injuries and conditions.</p></p>
