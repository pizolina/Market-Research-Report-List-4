<p><p>Peripheral Venous Catheters are classified into three main types based on their duration of use. Temporary catheters are used for a short duration, typically up to a week, for short-term medical needs. Short-term catheters are utilized for a longer period, up to a few weeks, for patients requiring extended medical treatment. Long-term catheters are designed for extended use, lasting months to years, for patients with chronic conditions or those in need of long-term therapy. Each type serves a specific purpose in providing safe and effective intravenous access.</p></p>
