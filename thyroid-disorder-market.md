<p><p>Thyroid disorders are conditions that affect the thyroid gland, leading to either overactivity (hyperthyroidism) or underactivity (hypothyroidism). Hyperthyroidism is characterized by an overproduction of thyroid hormones, resulting in symptoms like weight loss, rapid heartbeat, and anxiety. Hypothyroidism, on the other hand, occurs when the thyroid gland does not produce enough hormones, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and depression. Both conditions require proper diagnosis and treatment to manage symptoms and prevent complications.</p></p>
