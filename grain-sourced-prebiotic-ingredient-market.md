<p><p>Grain-sourced prebiotic ingredients are categorized into different types based on their composition and sources. These types include oligosaccharides, inulin, polydextrose, and others. Oligosaccharides are short-chain carbohydrates found in grains like wheat and oats, while inulin is a soluble fiber derived from sources like chicory root. Polydextrose is a synthetic prebiotic made from dextrose and sorbitol. Other grain-sourced prebiotic ingredients may include resistant starches and arabinoxylans that promote gut health and support the growth of beneficial bacteria.</p></p>
