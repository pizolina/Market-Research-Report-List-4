<p><p>Power transmission lines and towers are classified into three main types based on the voltage they carry: high tension, extra high tension, and ultra high tension. High tension lines typically carry voltages up to 230 kV, while extra high tension lines carry voltages between 230 kV and 800 kV. Ultra high tension lines carry voltages exceeding 800 kV. These different voltage levels determine the capacity and efficiency of the power transmission system, with higher tension lines being able to transmit larger amounts of electricity over longer distances.</p></p>
