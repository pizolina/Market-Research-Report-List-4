<p><p>Floating cranes can be classified into different types based on their hull design. Single hull vessels are traditional floating cranes with a single hull structure. Catamarans have two hulls parallel to each other, providing stability and increased lifting capacity. Semi-submersible floating cranes partially submerge their hulls underwater for added stability. Other types of floating cranes may include unique designs that combine different hull structures or have specialized features for specific lifting operations. Each type offers distinct advantages and capabilities for various maritime construction and cargo handling tasks.</p></p>
