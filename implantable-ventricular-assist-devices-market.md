<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are mechanical pumps used to support heart function in patients with advanced heart failure. Different types of VADs are available, including Left Ventricular Assist Devices (LVADs) which assist the left side of the heart, Right Ventricular Assist Devices (RVADs) which assist the right side of the heart, and Biventricular Assist Devices (BIVADs) which assist both sides. Each type of VAD is designed to improve blood flow, reduce symptoms of heart failure, and improve the overall quality of life for patients awaiting a heart transplant or as a long-term treatment option.</p></p>

<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are used in three main applications in the market. Bridge-to-transplant (BTT) involves using VADs as a temporary solution for patients waiting for a heart transplant. Destination Therapy (DT) is the long-term use of VADs for patients who are not eligible for a heart transplant. Other applications include bridge to recovery or candidacy for transplant in patients with potentially reversible heart conditions. These applications cater to different patient needs and provide a life-saving solution for those with advanced heart failure.</p></p>
