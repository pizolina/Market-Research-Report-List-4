<p><p>Thin film photovoltaic modules are classified into different types based on the materials used in their construction. Amorphous Silicon (a-Si) modules are made from non-crystalline silicon, Cadmium Telluride (CdTe) modules utilize a compound of cadmium and tellurium, and Copper Indium Gallium Selenide (CIS/CIGS) modules are composed of a mix of copper, indium, gallium, and selenium. Other types of thin film modules may include materials such as organic photovoltaic cells or dye-sensitized solar cells. Each type offers unique advantages and characteristics for generating solar power.</p></p>
