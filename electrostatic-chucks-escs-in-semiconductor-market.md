<p><p>Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are used in semiconductor manufacturing to securely hold wafers during processes like etching and deposition. There are two main types in the market: Coulomb Type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type. Coulomb Type ESCs use electrostatic forces to secure the wafer in place, while JR Type ESCs utilize a combination of Coulomb forces and Van der Waals forces for stronger holding power. Both types are essential components in semiconductor processing, providing reliable and stable wafer positioning.</p></p>
