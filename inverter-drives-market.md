<p><p>Inverter drives are commonly categorized into three types: eddy current drives, DC drives, and AC drives. Eddy current drives use magnetic fields to control speed, making them ideal for applications requiring precise speed regulation. DC drives convert AC power to DC power, offering smooth control over speed and torque. AC drives, also known as variable frequency drives, regulate motor speed by adjusting the frequency of the input power. Each type of drive has its own unique features and applications in various industries.</p></p>
